// The only rule

Build a simple product that solves a real problem. Not 10 features. Not "the everything app." Just one thing people actually need.

STEP 01 First thing to do

STOP IMPRESSING CT

Nobody cares yet. That's okay. Stop optimizing for attention you haven't earned. Start optimizing for a product that earns it.

✕  STOP doing this
  • Building a fancy landing page first
  • Talking about your token before your product
  • "Revolutionary protocol" threads with no demo
  • Chasing followers before you have users
  • Fake engagement loops — "GM fam" culture
  • 10 features at once
✓  DO this instead
  • Talk to 10 real people about their problem
  • Write the product brief before the code
  • Deploy something to devnet in week 1
  • Post a raw screenshot, not a polished render
  • Ask for feedback, not likes
  • Ship one thing completely

STEP 03 The real-world problem to solve

YOUR PROJECT: REMITCHAIN

You're in SuperteamNG. Nigeria sends over $20 billion in remittances yearly. The average fee is 8–12%. That's billions stolen from families. Solana can fix this in a way no bank ever will.

RemitChain
// Cross-border transfers for the unbanked. Under $0.01. Under 1 second.

A worker in the UK sends £200 to their mum in Lagos. Western Union takes £18 and 3 days. RemitChain takes £0.001 and 0.4 seconds. The receiver cashes out via a local agent or M-Pesa. No bank account needed. This is the product.

// MVP scope — one thing, completely done
  • Sender connects Phantom, inputs amount + recipient phone number
  • USDC locks into on-chain escrow via Anchor program (PDA)
  • Receiver gets a claim link / QR code via SMS or WhatsApp
  • Receiver redeems through local agent OR direct to their wallet
  • That's it. Four steps. Ship that. Nothing else.

How to actually build RemitChain, step by step:

1
Write the Anchor Program — on-chain logic only
Two instructions: initiate_transfer (locks USDC into a PDA escrow) and claim_transfer (recipient signs to unlock). Add cancel_transfer for expired claims. Deploy to devnet. Test with Solana CLI. Nothing else yet.
Rust Anchor SPL Token / USDC
2
Build the sender interface
React app. Phantom wallet connect. Input: USD amount + recipient identifier. Button: "Send". Shows Solscan TX link on confirmation. That's the whole sender flow for MVP.
React TypeScript @solana/web3.js
3
Build the claim page (mobile-first)
A single mobile page accessible via link or QR. Shows the amount waiting. Two options: "Connect wallet to claim" or "Find a local agent." This is what the receiver sees. Keep it stupid simple.
React Phantom Mobile
4
Test with real people in SuperteamNG
Send $2 of devnet USDC to 5 community members. Watch them try to claim it. Record what breaks. That feedback is worth more than 100 hours of solo building.
Devnet
5
Live demo — real money, mainnet
Send $5 USDC on mainnet during your demo video. Show the Solscan link. The transaction finalizes while you're still talking. That 0.4 seconds IS your pitch. Let the blockchain speak.
Mainnet Solscan
STEP 05 Building is not enough

DISTRIBUTION IS THE PRODUCT

A dead product with clean code is still a dead product. Distribution isn't marketing — it's survival. Start this on day one, not after you ship.

🏆
Superteam Earn Bounties — superteam.fun/earn
Get paid to build sub-components of your idea. Bounties = real feedback + money + reputation in the ecosystem. Do at least one bounty before the hackathon.
🎙
SuperteamNG Community Calls
Show your demo on a community call. 5 minutes of real builders seeing your product live is worth 500 Twitter impressions. Ask for brutal feedback. You need it.
🐦
Twitter / X — Build in Public
Post weekly: what you built, what broke, what a user said. Raw screenshots over polished graphics. Tag @superteamNG, @solana, @colosseum. One real build thread beats 30 GM posts.
🤝
Partnerships — Early & Scrappy
DM one remittance company, one local fintech, or one diaspora community group. You don't need a partnership deck — you need a working demo and a honest conversation. Start there.
👤
Real Users Giving Real Feedback
Find 10 Nigerians in the UK diaspora on Reddit, LinkedIn, or Facebook groups. Tell them you're building something that saves them 10% on every transfer home. They will test it. This is your beta.
STEP 06 Not branding — consistency

CONTENT = TRUST

People don't just support products anymore. They support builders. Show the journey, not just the destination.

✕  Don't post this
  • Polished brand graphics with no substance
  • Vague announcements: "Something big coming..."
  • Engagement bait: "RT to win SOL"
  • Thread about crypto in general, not your product
  • Metrics that don't matter: followers, likes
✓  Post this instead
  • What you shipped this week (with screenshot)
  • A mistake you made and how you fixed it
  • Direct user feedback, verbatim
  • A product update — even small ones
  • One lesson from shipping: no fluff, one insight
